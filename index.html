Mind+ - A Digital Companion for Academic Stress Management

Mind+ is an academic research initiative focused on supporting university students through intelligent stress detection, behavioral monitoring, and private digital assistance. The system is designed to improve early intervention and emotional resilience in academic life.

System Abstract

Mind+ is an intelligent digital companion designed to help university students monitor, understand, and manage academic stress. The system integrates self-monitoring, machine learning-based stress prediction, adaptive visualization, and anonymous counseling into a unified platform.
Unlike traditional wellness applications, Mind+ focuses specifically on academic stress and provides proactive, personalized support based on individual behavioral patterns.

Key Highlights:

  • Predicts stress levels 3–5 days in advance
  • Provides personalized stress insights
  • Uses adaptive heatmap visualization
  • Enables anonymous mental health support
  • Integrates voice journaling and community support

Core System Components

1. Speech-Based Reflective Support Module

A voice journaling system that enables users to record reflections. It analyzes voice entries to detect stress and emotional patterns. It also integrates a community support feature that matches users with peer groups based on identified stress categories, enabling meaningful interactions.

2. Self-Monitoring and Behavioral Tracking Module

Collects daily inputs such as stress level, sleep patterns, and energy levels. Acts as the primary data collection layer. Includes real-time coping mechanisms with an adaptive audio relaxation system (rain, forest, white noise), where tempo and pitch dynamically adjust based on stress levels using auditory entrainment.

3. Hybrid Stress Forecasting Module

A machine learning-based system that predicts short-term stress trends (3 to 5 days ahead). Uses behavioral data and models such as clustering, LSTM, and Random Forest to enable proactive stress management and personalized insights.

4. Anonymous Counseling Interface

A real-time, text-based support system using NLP to simulate therapeutic conversations. Allows students to seek help anonymously, ensuring privacy, reducing stigma, and providing immediate emotional support.
